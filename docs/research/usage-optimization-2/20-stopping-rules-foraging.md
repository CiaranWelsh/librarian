# Information Foraging, Stopping Rules, and Satisficing: When an Agent Should Stop Retrieving

**Sources:** Pirolli & Card, "Information Foraging" (*Psychological Review* 106(4), 1999) and Pirolli, *Information Foraging Theory* (OUP, 2007); Charnov's Marginal Value Theorem (1976); Browne, Pitts & Wetherbe, "Cognitive Stopping Rules for Terminating Information Search in Online Tasks" (*MIS Quarterly* 31(1), 2007) and Browne & Pitts (*OBHDP* 95(2), 2004); Marchionini, "Exploratory Search: From Finding to Understanding" (*CACM* 49(4), 2006); Bates, "berrypicking" (1989); FLARE (Jiang et al., EMNLP 2023, arXiv:2305.06983); Self-RAG (Asai et al., ICLR 2024); Stop-RAG (arXiv:2510.14337); EviOmni / "rational evidence via RL" (arXiv:2507.15586); "Toward Optimal Search and Retrieval for RAG" (arXiv:2411.07396); "Inference Scaling for Long-Context RAG" (arXiv:2410.04343).

## 1. The foraging frame: stop a patch when its marginal rate drops below the average

Information Foraging Theory (IFT) recasts search as optimal foraging: an agent works *information patches* (here, a librarian result set or a chunk's breadcrumb neighbourhood), and the within-patch gain function *decelerates* — each additional chunk read yields less new evidence. Charnov's **Marginal Value Theorem (MVT)** gives the optimal leave-time t\*: **quit the current patch when the slope of its within-patch gain function falls to the environment's average rate of gain R** (the tangent-line construction). Two consequences transfer directly to the librarian: (a) lower **between-patch cost** (cheap re-querying, fast embedding) pushes R up and makes the optimal strategy *more patches, less depth per patch* — the "opportunistic buffet, not banquet" prediction; (b) richer patches (better top-k, reranking) raise within-patch profitability and let R approach that profitability asymptotically. The actionable inversion: **the more useful one librarian search is, the sooner you should stop reading it and either answer or re-query.** This is the theoretical grounding under our Round-1 finding that k=20 retrieves but a small reranked set answers — and under PaperQA2's measured **1.26 searches/question**.

## 2. Humans don't optimise — they satisfice with discrete stopping rules, and the rule is task-dependent

Browne, Pitts & Wetherbe's online-search experiment (115 participants, three task types) is the most directly relevant empirical work. People do not compute MVT; they fire one of a small set of **cognitive stopping rules**: *mental-list* (stop when a checklist of criteria is satisfied), *magnitude threshold* (stop when a target amount of evidence accumulates), *representational stability* (stop when the mental model stops changing / new results stop shifting it), *difference/diminishing-returns* (stop when marginal new info drops), and *single-criterion / take-the-best*. **Crucially, the rule used depends on the task** — and later work (UNT 2016) found professional/structured tasks favour **a-priori** rules (decide the budget before searching), while open personal tasks favour **in-process** rules (decide during the search). This is the empirical backbone for the present round's central hypothesis: *the optimal stopping rule for the librarian is task-conditioned, not global.* Browne & Pitts (2004) add the design/choice split: **design/synthesis** tasks stop on *sufficiency for structuring* (breadth, coverage), **choice/lookup** tasks stop on *convergence* (one good answer) — distinct stop signals for distinct work.

## 3. Task taxonomy: lookup vs. learn/investigate predicts search trajectory shape

Marchionini separates **lookup** (discrete fact, one well-formed query, single patch) from **learn** and **investigate** (exploratory, multi-iteration, browsing-driven, sensemaking). Bates' **berrypicking** shows that in exploratory work the *query itself evolves* — "bit-at-a-time" retrieval across a looping, meandering path, not linear refinement toward a fixed target. Mapping to our task families: maths/API/single-science-fact lookups sit at the left end (one search, hard-stop, k small, depth-first into the one best chunk); literature synthesis / 4D-STEM-style investigation sits at the right end (multiple evolving queries, breadth across distinct sources, breadcrumb traversal as the "follow-the-berry" move). The empirical signature is measurable: exploratory tasks run longer and issue more, reformulated queries.

## 4. Modern agentic stopping: confidence-, sufficiency-, and value-based signals (with the over-retrieval warning)

The LLM literature has rediscovered all three IFT/Browne signals as engineering mechanisms. **FLARE** triggers retrieval on *token-level low confidence* (a calibration-based, in-process rule) — but is noted to **over-retrieve** and to give *no benefit on short-text tasks*, the direct analogue of "don't multi-search a lookup." **Self-RAG** trains reflection tokens to decide retrieve/don't-retrieve and to *not retrieve on non-knowledge-intensive instructions* (a necessity-estimation gate; cf. RetrievalQA benchmark). **EviOmni's "4R"** makes *right-timing early-stop* explicit: stop once sufficient rational evidence is gathered, vs. Search-R1's redundant continued searching. **Stop-RAG** formalises stopping as a finite-horizon MDP with a learned Q-network — evidence that "when to stop" is a *distinct, learnable control problem*, separate from retrieval quality. Google's **Sufficient-Context agent** reads the retrieved chunks and emits insufficient/sufficient plus a gap reason — a deployable mental-list rule. The recurring failure modes name the cost of getting it wrong: *retrieval thrash, tool-call cascades, context bloat, confident-wrong convergence.*

## 5. The diminishing-returns numbers (where to put the stop)

Empirically the within-patch gain curve saturates early and then inverts. "Toward Optimal Search and Retrieval for RAG" (arXiv:2411.07396): accuracy gains are large from k=1→5, **marginal from top-10→top-20**, plateauing ~5–10 docs; optimum is model/task-dependent (LLaMA best at k=4–5 for ASQA/NQ but k=10 for QAMPARI). **Recall keeps climbing toward ~1k docs while NDCG and answer quality plateau or fall** — recall and downstream quality diverge, and citation *precision* peaks at much lower k than correctness (more docs → more extraneous citations). The inverted-U ("too little = missing info, too much = distraction") is reproduced across benchmarks. **Filtering/reranking changes the optimum**: with a noise-suppressing filter, performance keeps rising to k=30; without it, it peaks at 10 then drops below the k=5 level. This matches PaperQA2 (retrieve 30, answer on ~5) and our k=20-retrieve / k=8-value-point.

## 6. Actionable implications for task-conditioned librarian experiments

1. **Define and benchmark a per-task stopping rule against the ~1.26-search baseline.** Lookup/maths/coding: hard-stop at one search (FLARE's short-text null result; Marchionini lookup). Synthesis/investigation: allow multi-search with breadcrumb traversal as the berrypicking move. Measure searches-per-task and answer quality jointly.
2. **Operationalise MVT as a marginal-gain stop.** Track *new distinct evidence per additional chunk read*; stop the patch when marginal new coverage falls below a threshold (the diminishing-returns rule). This is the testable form of "tangent = average rate R."
3. **Make the librarian's confidence label the stop/continue gate, per task.** Encode Browne's *magnitude-threshold* (≥N corroborating chunks) for choice/lookup and *representational-stability* (re-query stops changing the answer) for synthesis; compare against a fixed-budget baseline. Pair with a *necessity gate* (Self-RAG/RetrievalQA): for some tasks, the right number of searches is **zero**.
4. **Decouple retrieve-k from answer-k by task and sweep independently.** Hypothesis from §5: broad-retrieve/narrow-answer (e.g., 20→5) wins for synthesis; small symmetric k (≈5→3) wins for lookup. Add a reran/filter layer and re-measure the optimum (filtering shifts saturation from ~10 to ~30).
5. **Track precision separately from recall/coverage per task.** Citation precision and answer correctness saturate at different k; the abstention contract already pinned hallucination at 0%, so the next per-task axis is recall without re-introducing unsupported claims.
6. **Instrument the agentic failure modes as guardrails.** Cap search count to prevent thrash/cascades; monitor context-bloat-vs-quality; flag confident-wrong convergence on single-source synthesis (encode source-diversity ≥ M distinct books/papers as a complementary stop signal).
